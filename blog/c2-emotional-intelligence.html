Refections on Ch. 4 of Search Inside Yourself

Did you connect with any of the analogies for the mind?

Surprisingly I found these analogies quite easy to grasp and easy to understand. The first analogy was a visual one showing two versions of an image - one was grainy and smudgy, the other was clear and fine-grained. This very clearly showed the meanings of resolution and vividness. The next analogy of a flag as the mind and a flagpole as mindfulness was also an easy one to recall. Finally the idea of letting thoughts or emotions pass as if "being written on water" is a simple but beautiful way to put it.

What learnings did you take from the chapter? Well the three main points of: Emotional awareness; Accurate self-assessment; and Self-confidence. The follow sections elaborated on possible ways to acheive the first, to develop the second, and finally result in the third. I think the basic idea of using "I feel" instead of "I am" is a very useful shorthand way to really start modifying ones behaviour. I am a little sceptical on how one can know if one's self assessments are accurate or just delusions! How did you find the body scanning? Not easy. It's hard to retain a meditative state while trying to go through a checklist! (At least that's what it felt like!) This must take quite a while to achieve, and it may be hard to learn just from a book alone.
