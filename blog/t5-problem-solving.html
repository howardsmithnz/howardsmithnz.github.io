Technical Blog - Sprint 8

Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you

1. Got blocked on a simple problem
What was the problem?

I've often become blocked on simple coding tasks because I've had too many things on the go.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

The usual solution is to have a quick break then come back and simplify things. (i.e. ignore the other tasks for a while, close windows on the PC, clean up the desk/room, etc).

How did you feel throughout the process?

Initially confused but eventually clarity creeps in.

What did you learn?

That humans have a very limited capacity to multi-task (unlike computers)

2. Solved a problem in an elegant way
What was the problem?

When teaching English I found that students were to fixated on technical aspects of language but were missing the basic point of language learning - communication.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I thought widely about the resources that myself and the students could easily access and understand. I then selected the most accessible of these.

How did you feel throughout the process?

Pleased that my direction was correct but also aware that the idea required some pitching to various stakeholders.

What did you learn?

To trust my instincts!

Explain to your non-tech friend using the JS Array docs as a starting point, describe what these functions do in your own words:

.map()

This simply takes a function and applies it to every member of the data set. The result is a new data set.

.filter()

This gives us all the members of a data set that pass a test

.reduce()

This reduces all members of a data set to one data item.

In a 20 minute timebox, have a go at explaining how recursion compares to iteration (In your own words)

Recursion is a process where a function may call itself (usually with different arguments). It must have a "base case" or terminating point otherwise it will try to continue for ever. Recursion will be a natural fit for some problems (such as tree traversal) but may have performance issues.

Iteration means repeating the same task a set number of times or until a true condition becomes false. Iteration often works faster than recursion however it may lead to the logic of the problem becoming obfuscated.
